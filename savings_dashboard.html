Dave: You're asking for a visual overview of investment options available to you as a 16-year-old. Great question! As a 16-year-old, your investment options are more limited than adults, but you still have several solid paths forward: **Direct Investment Options:** - **Custodial Brokerage Account** – Your parent/guardian opens an account in your name. You can invest in stocks, ETFs, and mutual funds. - **Roth IRA (with earned income)** – If you have a job, you can open a Roth IRA and contribute up to your earned income or $7,000/year (whichever is less). Tax-free growth is huge at your age! - **High-Yield Savings Account (HYSA)** – Safe, liquid, and earns 4-5% APY. Great for emergency funds or short-term goals. - **Certificates of Deposit (CDs)** – Low-risk, fixed returns (currently 4-5% for short terms). **Indirect Options:** - **529 Education Savings Plan** – Your parents can set this up for your college costs (tax-advantaged). - **Peer-to-Peer Lending** – Some platforms allow teens with parental consent. - **Fractional Shares** – Apps like Fidelity or Schwab let you buy partial stocks with small amounts. **What You Can't Do Yet:** - Options trading, margin accounts, or day trading (typically require age 18+) Let me create a visual dashboard to show you these options with comparisons: ```html Teen Investment Options Dashboard
